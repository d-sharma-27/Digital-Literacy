## Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

UPI/Online Payment Fraud is a common type of cybercrime in which fraudsters trick individuals into transferring money or sharing sensitive banking details. This type of fraud has increased significantly with the rise of digital payment platforms in India.
A typical scenario begins when the victim receives a call, message, or link from someone pretending to be a bank official, delivery agent, or customer support representative. The fraudster may claim that the victim needs to verify their account, receive a refund, or complete a transaction.
They then send a fake payment request or ask the victim to share their UPI PIN or OTP. In some cases, the victim is asked to click on a malicious link or scan a QR code. Once the victim approves the request or shares confidential information, the fraudster gains access and quickly transfers money from the victim’s bank account.
Students, elderly individuals, and people unfamiliar with digital payments are the most common targets of such scams. The consequences can be serious, including financial loss, stress, and loss of trust in digital platforms. In many cases, recovering the lost money is difficult if the fraud is not reported immediately.
This case highlights the importance of awareness and caution while using online payment systems.
